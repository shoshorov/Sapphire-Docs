Accordions group and categorize related content in a compact and space-efficient manner. They can have multiple levels of nested content, helping users navigate through complex information in a structured manner.

## Anatomy
<Image src={accordionAnatomy} alt="Accordion anatomy diagram" class="rounded-corners border" loading="eager"  />
1. **Header**: contains the section title and is control for revealing the panel.
2. **Panel**: the section of content associated with an accordion header.
3. **Icons and buttons** (optional): provide additional functionality to the accordion.
4. **Arrow**: indicates if the panel is open or closed.

## When to use 

- To organize related information.
- To shorten pages and reduce scrolling when content is not crucial to read in full.
- When space is at a premium and long content cannot be displayed all at once, like on a mobile interface or in a side panel.

## When not to use 

- When organizing large amounts of information that can be nested.
- If a user is likely to read all of the content, then don't use an accordion as it adds the burden of an extra click.

## Examples

Click the accordions below to expand/collapse the accordion content.

To render an accordion that's expanded by default:

- add the `.show` class on the `.sp-accordion-collapse` element.
- drop the `.collapsed` class from the `.sp-accordion-button` element and set its `aria-expanded` attribute to true.
